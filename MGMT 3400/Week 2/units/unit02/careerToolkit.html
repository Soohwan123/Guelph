Dissecting a Job Ad

Before you can even apply to a job, you need to first understand what the employer is looking for. This involves critically evaluating the job ad, also known as a job posting. Dissecting a job posting or ad is a critical skill because you need to be able to tailor your application to specific opportunities effectively. This involves analyzing the job requirements, responsibilities, and qualifications outlined in the posting, to gain insight into the employer's needs and expectations.

There are several techniques for dissecting job postings, including identifying keywords and key phrases, understanding the organization’s culture and values, and aligning your skills and experiences with the position requirements.

Identify Keywords and Phrases

When you read a job ad, you need to identify keywords and phrases. You should be asking yourself:

  • What specific phrases are used in relation to the skills and qualifications required for this role?
  • Are there any specific technical or industry-related terms mentioned?
  • What qualifications and experiences are listed as "preferred" or "desired"?
  • Are there any specific certifications or qualifications mentioned?
  • What soft skills or attributes are emphasized, such as communication, teamwork, or problem-solving?
  • Are there any specific tools, software, or technologies mentioned?
  • Are there any specific educational requirements or degrees mentioned?
  • Are there any keywords or phrases that are repeated multiple times throughout the job posting?

Culture and Values

Remember that when you are looking for a new job, you are also evaluating the organization and whether it aligns with your values and priorities. Understanding these values also allows you to further tailor your application package to align with the organization and employer expectations. For this, you may need to look beyond the job ad and do some research on other areas of the organization’s website. Look for key documents like strategic plans and mission statements to learn more about them.

  • How does the company describe its culture? Is it collaborative, innovative, or customer-focused?
  • What values does the company emphasize? For example, are integrity, diversity, or sustainability mentioned?
  • How does the company prioritize employee well-being and development?
  • Are there any specific initiatives or programs mentioned that reflect the organization’s values, such as diversity and inclusion efforts or corporate social responsibility projects?
  • Are there any clues about the organization’s leadership style or management philosophy?
  • Does the company promote a culture of continuous learning and growth?
  • Are there any testimonials or quotes from current employees that provide insights into the company culture and values?

Position Requirements

Position requirements tell you more about the hard facts about the role. You’ll notice there is some overlap or similar focus to key phrases and words. However, the difference between looking for keywords and position requirements lies in the type of questions and how you look at the ad or posting. The focus here is on understanding the expectations and needs, whereas keywords provide you with language to use in your own application.

  • How much experience is required, and what level of expertise is expected?
  • Are there any specific educational requirements, certifications, or licenses needed?
  • What are the primary responsibilities and duties associated with the position?
  • Are there any preferred or desired qualifications that could enhance candidacy?
  • How do the requirements align with your own skills, experiences, and career goals?
  • Are there opportunities for growth and advancement within the role?
  • What level of autonomy or decision-making authority is associated with the position?
  • How do the position requirements align with the organization’s goals and objectives?

Developing an Application Package

After you have evaluated a job ad and decided to apply, you need to develop a tailored application package. Most commonly, this involves developing a resume and cover letter.

The Resume

Writing an effective resume is a strategic process that involves showcasing your skills, experiences, and achievements in a clear and compelling manner. Begin by crafting a concise summary statement that highlights your career objectives and key qualifications. Then, organize your resume with sections such as education, work experience, skills, and achievements, using bullet points to articulate your accomplishments in each role. Tailor your resume to the specific job you're applying for by incorporating keywords and phrases from the job posting, and quantify your achievements whenever possible to demonstrate your impact. Finally, ensure your resume is visually appealing and easy to read by using a clean and professional layout, concise language, and consistent formatting throughout. By following these guidelines, you can create a resume that effectively markets your skills and experiences.

The Cover Letter

Many job postings will also ask for a cover letter. If the posting asks for a cover letter, include one. Following the instructions from the ad is an easy way to show you can follow instructions. Writing an effective cover letter is an opportunity to showcase your personality, enthusiasm, and qualifications in a personalized way that complements your resume. Start by addressing the letter to the hiring manager or recruiter by name, if possible, and introduce yourself with a brief opening paragraph that expresses your interest in the position and highlights your relevant experience. In the main paragraphs, you can dive deeper into specific examples of your skills, experiences, and accomplishments that align with the job requirements, providing concrete evidence of your fit for the role. Use the cover letter as a chance to demonstrate your knowledge of the company and its industry, and explain why you're excited about the opportunity to contribute to their success. Finally, close the letter with a strong concluding paragraph that reiterates your interest in the position and invites further discussion, and thank the reader for their time and consideration. The goal is to use the cover letter to make a memorable impression and increase your chances of standing out to potential employers.

Preparing for an Interview

In an ideal world, your tailored application package lands you an interview—congratulations! Now it is time to prepare. Preparing for an interview involves thorough research, thoughtful reflection, and strategic reflection to present yourself effectively to potential employers. Return to your research from when you developed your application package on the organization, its culture, values, and recent news or developments. This will give you a deeper understanding of the employer’s operations and goals. You also need to reflect on your experiences, skills, and accomplishments, and prepare specific examples to demonstrate your qualifications for the role. Practice answering common interview questions, focusing on articulating your strengths, achievements, and alignment with the job requirements. Additionally, anticipate questions about your resume and be prepared to discuss your career trajectory and motivations.

On the day of the interview, ensure you are well-rested, professionally dressed, and arrive on time. If the interview is online, make sure that you have a strong internet connection and that there are no distractions and your background is professional. You may want to bring copies of your resume, a list of references, and any relevant documents or portfolio materials depending on the type of job. By investing time and effort into thorough preparation, you can approach the interview with confidence and increase your chances of making a positive impression on the interviewer.

Resume Writing 101

As someone who has been on many hiring committees, I cannot tell you how often poor resumes come across my desk. Resumes that include information about a different job opportunity, that are difficult to read, are too broad, the formatting is messy, or it includes information and experiences that seem irrelevant. Recruiters spend an average of 6 seconds doing an initial scan of a resume – if they notice these kinds of errors it is an easy way for them to shortlist – without your application in the mix.

It is important that you provide a well formatted, concise, and tailored resume when applying for a job. While something like formatting may seem minor to you, it demonstrates that you do not have attention to detail. In addition, most workplaces require the use of Microsoft Office – if you can’t format your own resume properly, this leaves doubts about whether you can format a report to a client in a professional manner.

Fortunately, the University of Guelph offers many resources to help you. The Experiential Learning Hub offers many resources and services to help guide you. This includes meeting with a Peer Helper you will review your resume and cover letter, or do a mock interview with you.

Review the Resume Writing 101 resource from the Experiential Learning Hub on how to write your resume. You need to follow these details for your assignments.

The Art of the Cover Letter

Similar to the resume, you do not want your cover letter to be generic. As we will discuss more in the Career Advantage section, recruiters have started to screen out applicants who they think used AI to write their cover letter. AI tends to use the same language when asked similar prompts, so the cover letters will sound very similar. Since the purpose of the cover letter is to help you stand out, this defeats the purpose.

Artificial Intelligence and Technology in the Application Process

This section of each unit is going to focus on spotlighting something unique or unusual about the unit topic. This section is important because it highlights something practical and useful. These topics have been selected based on how much understanding this topic could positively impact your career exploration, and give you a competitive advantage in the workforce.

What is the role of AI and technology in the application and hiring process?

Technology has been evolving in such a way that organizations are increasingly using it to streamline their hiring processes. A key use has been the use of artificial intelligence to scan resumes and cover letters. This has only increased the importance of tailoring your resume to the job ad, as we discussed. However, not everyone has transitioned to these tools, especially as issue with biased algorithms have caused organizations to pause or hesitate in their use of these tools as the sole evaluator of applications. It would be a mistake to assume that a person will not be reading your application package.

The other use of technology that has been increasing is the use of asynchronous or pre-recorded video interviews in the first round of interviewing. In this type of interview, candidates respond to a series of interview questions either through written text or pre-recorded video, following straightforward instructions for recording their answers. Recruiters and hiring professionals have the flexibility to watch, evaluate, and compare these recorded video interviews at their convenience. Additionally, the video responses are readily shareable with other stakeholders involved in the hiring process, allowing them to review the interviews at their own pace.

What about the perspective of the applicant?

Organizations are not the only ones that can take advantage of changes in technology. Applicants are using tools to help them as well, and you can too! The popularity of large language models, like ChatGPT, has resulted in applicants using this tool to help them improve their resumes, prepare for interviews, and write cover letters. While these tools can be helpful, be aware that there are some challenges. First, there is understandably less critical thinking involved in developing the application package when you do not write it. This can be a challenge if you get to the interview stage because you are likely to have less familiarity with the job posting.

The other issue is that employers have noticed the use of ChatGPT and other similar tools. This is causing cover letters written with obvious AI generated, or even suspected AI generated language, to be de-prioritized in the selection process. Not developing a genuine or authentic approach to job applications remains a risk and could negatively impact your job search. For this course, the use of AI is prohibited, as the goal is to get you to use your own critical thinking skills in developing your application package.

What is the Career Advantage?

Technology is only going to continue to evolve and change. Being aware of recent trends and tools and how to use them responsibly is essential not only to give you a competitive advantage, but also to ensure we are behaving ethically.

For more information on AI and its responsible use in the hiring process, check out this article from VidCruiter: Why You Need an AI Framework for Recruiting.

When it comes to building your career toolkit:

  • Avoid: Thinking that technology and AI is a complete replacement for the human element of the hiring process. Overreliance on AI can cause problems for you later on in the hiring process, and misrepresentation of your skills can result in a quick, involuntary exit even if you are successful in getting a job offer.
  • Do: Balance the use of technology to enhance and promote responsible efficiencies and competitive advantages. People should come first, and you need critical media and technology literacy to ensure we are maintaining an inclusive and fair future in the workplace.
